# Global-Climate-Health-Analysis

Using Tableau Public and the dataset named Global Climate Health Impact Tracker (2015-2025) by Sohum Gokhale posted on Kaggle.com, I have done an analysis on the data in tableau. 

The tableau visualizations explore various different types of factors and issues impacting different regions and specific countries. Please download the raw file and view. 
Visualizations Covered within this Tableau analysis include:
1) Average Air Quality Index by Region (Bar Chart)
  > Insights: South Asia has the highest average in Air Quality index score being 154.71 (unhealthy) while South America has the lowest average Air Quality Index Score being 65.91 (moderate).
2) Heat Days by Region (Treemap)
  > Europe by far has the highest number of heat days at 424 heat days while Oceania has the lowest number of heat days at 77 heat days. 
3) Waterborne Disease by Country colored by Region (Bar Chart)
  > Bangladesh is leading in waterborne diease incidents at 15,757 incidents while the United Kingdom has the lowest number of waterborne disease incidents at 9,523 incidents.
4) Precipitation by Region (Bar Chart)
  > Europe, as a region, has the highest amount of precipitation (Germany having the highest precipitation) and Oceania having the least precipitation with 45,310mm.
5) Average Food Security Index by Country (Bar Chart)
  > In 2015, United Kingdom had an average food security index score of 99.09 indicating better food security on a scale of 100, and that same year, the Phillippines had an average food security index score of 86.38, which was the lowest score among the other countries present within the data. In 2025, the UK has a score of 98.95, while Phillippines remains at the bottom at 85.60.
